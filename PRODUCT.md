# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Albanian speakers in Albania, Kosovo, North Macedonia, and the diaspora, plus learners who want a short daily language ritual. Most sessions happen on a phone during a five-minute break; the primary task is to find one five-letter Albanian word without setup or an account.

## Product Purpose

FJALË is a daily and replayable Albanian word game. It treats the Albanian alphabet correctly, including its nine digraph letters, and turns each solved word into a small vocabulary moment. Success means the game feels instantly familiar, linguistically respectful, fair, and worth returning to each day.

## Brand Personality

Clever, lively, and grounded. The voice is concise Albanian with a warm wink, never childish or over-explained. The emotional goal is a quick moment of focus followed by earned delight and a quiet sense of connection to the language.

## Anti-references

Do not make this a flag-and-folklore souvenir, a noisy casino-style streak machine, an English Wordle reskin, or a generic game covered in coins, gems, pop-ups, and artificial urgency. Avoid color-only feedback, inaccessible motion, obscure answer words, and punitive dictionary rejection.

## Design Principles

1. **Treat Albanian as a first-class system.** Count `DH`, `GJ`, `LL`, `NJ`, `RR`, `SH`, `TH`, `XH`, and `ZH` as letters, preserve `Ë` and `Ç`, and explain the rule in plain language.
2. **Put the word before the chrome.** The board and keyboard are immediately usable; secondary features stay one quiet action away.
3. **Teach after the attempt.** Definitions, examples, and alphabet progress enrich the result without interrupting the solve.
4. **Make delight earned.** Celebration, the alphabet passport, and Besa badges respond to real progress instead of decorating every surface.
5. **Keep the game fair and transparent.** Hints are optional and marked in shared results; there is no currency, paywall, or hidden scoring rule.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Never communicate tile state by color alone; pair color with symbols and Albanian screen-reader announcements. Support full physical-keyboard play, 200% zoom, high contrast, dark mode, reduced motion, safe-area insets, and comfortable touch targets. Sound and vibration must remain optional. Standard Albanian is the shared daily baseline; any future regional words must be clearly labeled and native-reviewed.
