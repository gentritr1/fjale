## Product recap

FJALË is a daily and endless Albanian word game where all 36 letters are first-class tiles, including the nine digraphs. It adds a delayed semantic hint (Gjurmë), an opt-in no-hint promise (Besa), an Alphabet Passport, post-win definitions/examples, streaks and statistics, friend challenges, sharing, accessibility preferences, and offline PWA support.

## Overall verdict

**Good product fit, conditional launch.** The current build is an unusually strong controlled beta and a credible free Albanian cultural/learning ritual. It is not yet a durable public daily service because the 62-answer schedule repeats after 62 days, editorial trust has not been established with native reviewers across regions, and the product has no feedback or measurement loop.

- Current niche-product fit: **6.8/10**
- Potential after content and retention work: **8/10**
- Standalone monetized-startup fit: **about 4/10**
- Interface health: **33/40 — Good**

## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Does it look AI-generated? No.** The linguistic mechanics, Ë mark, Besa framing, Passport, scarce honey accent, flat layout, and symbol-paired tile states feel specific to the product. It avoids generic cream SaaS surfaces, card grids, gradient text, glass, excessive rounding, and ambient shadows.

The deterministic Impeccable scan returned `[]`: zero rule findings and zero false positives. Browser inspection did find one issue the static detector could not: the mobile hint-count badge is positioned against the page rather than its button, creating 4px of horizontal overflow at 390px and 320px.

## What is working

1. **Albanian is product behavior, not localization.** Dedicated digraph keys, atomic typing/deletion, optical sizing, and plain-language explanation create a better fit than translated clones.
2. **The game remains focused.** The board and keyboard dominate, while Passport, hints, statistics, and settings stay secondary.
3. **The peak-end moment is promising.** Definitions, examples, sharing, challenge links, and alphabet progress turn a win into a language moment.
4. **Accessibility is unusually deliberate.** State symbols accompany colors; keyboard play, focus rings, live announcements, native dialogs, dark/high-contrast themes, reduced motion, and persistence are present.

## Feature differentiation

| Feature | Verdict |
|---|---|
| **Gjurmë** | Useful and fair, especially for intermediate learners, but not a moat. It is supporting utility. |
| **Besa** | Memorable Albanian branding, but currently borderline gimmick because it only promises not to use an optional hint. It should become a meaningful hard mode or an automatic no-hint achievement. |
| **Alphabet Passport** | The strongest original idea. It expresses the 36-letter alphabet naturally, but the 62-word pool makes rare letters grindy and the completion path unpredictable. |

The real moat is **correct Albanian mechanics plus trusted editorial quality**.

## Cognitive load and emotional journey

Gameplay itself has low cognitive load. The completed state has moderate load: single focus, hierarchy, and progressive disclosure fail because result, share, challenge, replay, hint, Besa, and Passport remain visible together. The opening is confident; each guess builds useful tension; winning is satisfying. The ending then dilutes the peak by returning attention to stale pre-game helpers rather than leaving the player with the word and its meaning.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] The daily content runway and editorial trust are not launch-ready

**Why it matters:** the 62-answer schedule repeats after 62 days. A single obscure or regionally awkward daily word can damage trust faster than a visual defect. The Hunspell guess list is broad, not an editorial answer list.

**Fix:** build at least 365 curated answers, preferably 500–1,000; establish native review in Albania and Kosovo; score frequency/difficulty; reject obscure answers; verify metadata; add a report-word path; preserve a versioned schedule.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable harden`

### [P1] The vocabulary payoff is subordinated to pre-game utilities

**Why it matters:** the word meaning is the strongest reason this is more than an Albanian Wordle clone, yet desktop places it after hint/Besa/Passport and those helpers retain stale copy after completion.

**Fix:** move the result to the top of the secondary column at every breakpoint; collapse helpers into a completion summary such as “Pa gjurmë · Besa jo · +5 shkronja”; let the final emotional note be the word.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable layout`

### [P2] The distinctive mobile controls are least discoverable, and the hint badge is detached

**Why it matters:** mobile hides Gjurmë and Besa labels even though those are unfamiliar concepts. The hint countdown badge escapes its button and creates horizontal overflow.

**Fix:** position `.tool-button` as the badge containing block, keep compact labels or teach them once inline, and retest at 320px and 200% zoom.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable adapt`

### [P2] Endless mode lacks escape and statistics mix unlike sessions

**Why it matters:** a player cannot skip an unwanted active endless word. Practice/challenge wins can also inflate the same win rate and Besa totals used by daily play.

**Fix:** add “Fjalë tjetër” with confirmation only when progress exists; separate daily, practice, and challenge statistics; reduce rapid practice repetition.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable shape`

### [P2] Audience and reset promises are broader than the product

**Why it matters:** the current experience fits fluent speakers and intermediate/heritage learners, not beginners. A Tirana-time reset can display tomorrow’s Albanian date to North American diaspora users. There is no analytics or rejected-word feedback to learn where this hurts.

**Fix:** narrow the learner promise or add optional translation/pronunciation; explicitly explain the shared Tirana reset; instrument privacy-respecting activation, completion, hint, share, return, and rejection events.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable clarify`

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan — first-timer:** understands the board quickly, but the icon-only Besa control can remove hints before its meaning is visible. Post-game helper copy can promise an unlock that is no longer possible.

**Casey — distracted mobile user:** benefits from persistence and bottom-positioned input. Risks are 28–40px-wide alphabet keys, the detached hint badge, result/header occlusion, and a long post-win tail of secondary content.

**Arta — diaspora/heritage learner:** challenge links and post-word definitions are emotionally relevant, but Albanian-only explanations, obscure answers, and Tirana-time reset semantics make the learner/diaspora promise incomplete.

## Minor Observations

- “5 shkronja · 36 mundësi · 1 fjalë” can imply 36 possible words; “36 shkronja” is clearer.
- `00:00` looks like a broken timer; use “< 1 sek”.
- Malformed challenge links silently fall back to the daily game.
- The share grid’s two-symbol cells (`🟩✓`) may be visually bulky in messaging apps.
- The generic name FJALË competes with existing Fjalëz/FJALËZ products in search and naming.
- GPL-derived dictionary data makes a closed-source commercial direction awkward unless the lexicon is replaced.

## Questions to Consider

- Is the goal a beloved free Albanian ritual, a classroom/heritage-learning tool, or a standalone business? The same build cannot optimize all three equally.
- If vocabulary is the differentiator, why is its result queued behind utility modules?
- Is Besa important enough to become a real hard mode, or should no-hint success be awarded automatically?
- Should Passport completion depend on random play, or actively guide players toward missing letters?
